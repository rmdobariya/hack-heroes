The Journey of Sam, The Safe Surfer

Set clear rules and guidelines: Establish clear guidelines for internet usage and educate your child about the dangers of cyberbullying.

Recommendation for Taylor

Transform education into a captivating tale about 'Sam', a character who learns how to safely navigate the internet. Read the story of Sam, The Safe Surfer to your child. Discuss the story with your child, and relate it to their own internet usage.

Age: Younger children, in particular, respond well to storytelling. By presenting digital safety concepts in a story format, it becomes more accessible and relatable to them. Online behaviour: Through Sam's experiences and lessons in the story, children can draw parallels and gain insights into safe versus risky behaviours, adjusting their own online actions accordingly.

Parental involvement: Reading the story together fosters parental involvement. It also provides a structured platform for discussing online safety, making it easier for parents to broach the topic and relate it to real-life scenarios. Peer relationships: As stories often encompass interactions with other characters, discussing Sam's interactions can segue into discussions about the child's own online peer relationships.

Support system: The story can emphasise the importance of seeking guidance and support when faced with online challenges. Children can be encouraged to emulate Sam by leaning on their support systems.
